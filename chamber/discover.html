Demographics

Sioux Falls, SD is home to a population of 189k people,
from which 95.2% are citizens.
As of 2021, 8.71% of Sioux Falls,
SD residents were born outside of the country (16.5k people).

Education

In 2021, universities in Sioux Falls, SD awarded 2,277 degrees. The student population of Sioux Falls, SD in 2021 is skewed towards women, with 2,865 male students and 4,085 female students. Most students graduating from Universities in Sioux Falls, SD are White (1,953 and 87.5%), followed by Hispanic or Latino (80 and 3.58%), Black or African American (56 and 2.51%), and Asian (54 and 2.42%). The largest universities in Sioux Falls, SD by number of degrees awarded are Southeast Technical College (878 and 38.6%), Augustana University (688 and 30.2%), and University of Sioux Falls (532 and 23.4%). The most popular majors in Sioux Falls, SD are Registered Nursing (195 and 8.56%), General Business Administration & Management (181 and 7.95%), and Licensed Practical & Vocational Nurse Training (116 and 5.09%). The median tuition cost in Sioux Falls, SD for private four year colleges is $19,550.

Economy

The economy of Sioux Falls, SD employs 106k people. The largest industries in Sioux Falls, SD are Health Care & Social Assistance (18,931 people), Retail Trade (13,606 people), and Manufacturing (11,272 people), and the highest paying industries are Mining, Quarrying, & Oil & Gas Extraction ($93,750), Professional, Scientific, & Technical Services ($68,060), and Utilities ($60,489). Males in South Dakota have an average income that is 1.29 times higher than the average income of females, which is $48,431. The income inequality in South Dakota (measured using the Gini index) is 0.439, which is lower than than the national average.

Health

92% of the population of Sioux Falls, SD has health coverage, with 58.6% on employee plans, 9.86% on Medicaid, 9.66% on Medicare, 12.2% on non-group plans, and 1.67% on military or VA plans. Primary care physicians in Minnehaha County, SD see 1006 patients per year on average, which represents a 5.09% decrease from the previous year (1060 patients). Compare this to dentists who see 1804 patients per year, and mental health providers who see 502 patients per year.
